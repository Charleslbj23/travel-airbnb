Argument 1: Listing Concentration

Airbnb listings in Los Angeles are not evenly distributed — they cluster in tourism-oriented neighborhoods, functioning as a concentrated accommodation market rather than a dispersed home-sharing network.

Where Airbnb clusters, and how that reshapes place

Airbnb listings in Los Angeles are not evenly distributed across the city but instead show a clear spatial concentration in tourism-oriented neighborhoods. The geographic distribution of listings reveals major clusters of short-term rentals across the region. The largest concentration appears in Long Beach with approximately 1,906 listings (4.18% of all listings), followed by Hollywood with 1,758 listings (3.86%) and Venice with 1,529 listings (3.35%). Additional high-density areas include Santa Monica (1,243 listings), West Hollywood (1,219 listings), and Downtown Los Angeles (1,155 listings). These neighborhoods are already major visitor destinations because they are located near beaches, nightlife districts, cultural attractions, and the entertainment industry, making them particularly attractive locations for short-term rentals.

This spatial clustering suggests that Airbnb functions less as a dispersed "home-sharing" network and more as a tourism accommodation market concentrated in specific areas. Listings are not randomly distributed across residential neighborhoods but instead cluster where visitor demand and tourism infrastructure are strongest. In these areas, housing units can more easily be converted into short-term rentals for visitors.

Two geographies of commodification

The concentration of Airbnb listings in residential neighborhoods raises important questions about how housing is used within urban space. When large numbers of housing units are converted into short-term rentals in areas with strong visitor demand, these neighborhoods begin to function less as long-term residential communities and more as informal tourist zones. In this way, the spatial distribution of Airbnb listings shows how digital platforms interact with existing tourism geographies and contribute to the gradual transformation of residential housing into temporary accommodation for visitors.

Neighborhoods with the highest entire-home rates — Malibu (93.6%), Manhattan Beach (90.8%), Hollywood Hills West (90.9%) — are concentrated in wealthy coastal areas. By contrast, neighborhoods with lower entire-home rates — Exposition Park (12.1%), University Park (25.0%) — are characterized by private-room sharing, revealing two fundamentally different Airbnb economies operating within the same city.

Platform growth and the COVID-19 rupture

Airbnb's presence in LA grew steadily from 2014 through 2019, with new listing activations peaking right before the Home-Sharing Ordinance took full effect. COVID-19 caused a catastrophic collapse: new listing activations dropped from 210 in January 2020 to just 11 in April 2020 — a 95% decline. By 2024–2025, the platform surged past previous peaks, with listing activity reaching its highest levels in the dataset's 16-year window.
