Argument 2: Housing Commodification

The dominance of entire-home listings shows how residential housing units are increasingly converted into short-term accommodations for visitors, transforming homes into revenue-generating assets.

Airbnb and the commodification of housing

One of the most significant patterns in Airbnb listings across Los Angeles is the dominance of entire-home rentals, which make up the largest share of listings on the platform. Approximately 33,580 listings are categorized as entire homes, far exceeding private-room or shared-room listings. This distinction is important because an entire-home listing represents a complete housing unit that could otherwise be occupied by a long-term resident. Unlike room rentals, where hosts may rent out a spare bedroom while continuing to live in the property, entire-home listings convert full residential properties into short-term accommodation for visitors.

Entire-home rentals account for the largest share of Airbnb listings across every part of Los Angeles. In the City of Los Angeles, approximately 74.8% of listings are entire homes, compared to 72.7% in other cities and 71.9% in unincorporated areas. This pattern suggests that Airbnb is used primarily for renting complete housing units rather than spare rooms.

Entire-home dominance by area

The breakdown of listings by host type across the City of LA, other cities, and unincorporated areas shows that entire-home listings dominate in every area. This indicates that the conversion of whole residential properties — not room-sharing — is the primary function of the platform across LA County. Income context and housing as investment

Socioeconomic context from Census data helps illustrate where housing commodification is most visible. Areas with higher household incomes often coincide with places where Airbnb activity is particularly prominent. The Agoura Hills–Malibu area reports a median household income of approximately $169,991, substantially higher than many other communities in Los Angeles County. In these contexts, housing can more easily function as an investment asset capable of generating short-term rental revenue.

High-income areas like Agoura Hills–Malibu ($170K median) and South Bay Cities ($161K) have fewer total listings but the highest entire-home rates (89–91%). The largest CCD, Los Angeles, dominates in volume (23,613 listings) at $80K median income. Together, these patterns suggest that Airbnb's most intensive commodification occurs in wealthier communities. See Viz 5 on the Data Viz page for the full income vs. listing scatter plot.

Two Airbnb economies within one city

The contrast between neighborhoods reveals two distinct models of platform use. In wealthy coastal neighborhoods — Malibu (93.6%), Manhattan Beach (90.8%) — over 90% of listings are entire homes, reflecting a market where full properties are listed as investment assets. In lower-income neighborhoods — Exposition Park (12.1%), University Park (25.0%) — private room-sharing dominates, reflecting a very different economic relationship to the platform.

This divergence shows that Airbnb does not operate as a single uniform market across Los Angeles. Instead, it produces geographically distinct patterns of commodification that align closely with existing neighborhood income levels and housing conditions.
