Housing Commodification

Airbnb and the commodification of housing

One of the most significant patterns in Airbnb listings across Los Angeles is the dominance of entire-home rentals, which make up the largest share of listings on the platform. Approximately 33,580 listings are categorized as entire homes, far exceeding private-room or shared-room listings. This distinction is important because an entire-home listing represents a complete housing unit that could otherwise be occupied by a long-term resident. Unlike room rentals, where hosts may rent out a spare bedroom while continuing to live in the property, entire-home listings convert full residential properties into short-term accommodation for visitors.

Figure 2 — Entire Home vs. Private Room

Source: InsideAirbnb Los Angeles, December 2025

As shown in Figure 2, entire-home rentals account for the largest share of Airbnb listings across different parts of Los Angeles. In the City of Los Angeles, approximately 74.8% of listings are entire homes, compared to 72.7% in other cities and 71.9% in unincorporated areas. This pattern suggests that Airbnb is used primarily for renting complete housing units.

Socioeconomic context from Census data helps illustrate where this process is most visible. Areas with higher household incomes and educational attainment often coincide with places where Airbnb activity is particularly prominent. For example, the Agoura Hills–Malibu area reports a median household income of approximately $169,991, substantially higher than many other communities in Los Angeles County. In these contexts, housing can more easily function as an investment asset capable of generating short-term rental revenue. Together, these patterns suggest that Airbnb contributes to the commodification of housing by enabling entire residential properties to function as short-term rental assets.
