Airbnb & LA

How does Airbnb spatially shape and commodify communities in Los Angeles, and for whom?

Start with Background Jump to Listing Concentration

Why Los Angeles?

Los Angeles is a city well-defined by tourism, entertainment, and housing disparity — making it an ideal case study for examining how short-term rental platforms reshape residential communities. In cities like LA, where tourism and housing inequality already overlap, the question of whether Airbnb primarily benefits local residents or encourages professionalized hosting by investors is especially pressing.

LA's combination of globally recognized tourist neighborhoods like Venice, Hollywood, and Santa Monica; an acute affordable housing crisis; and one of the country's largest and most diverse populations makes it a uniquely revealing lens for studying how platform economies interact with long-term community structures.

How we approach the question

A spatial and community-centered lens.

InsideAirbnb provides a high-resolution view of platform-generated housing supply, while the American Community Survey offers a structural view of neighborhood demographics and socioeconomic conditions. By spatially joining listings to census tracts, we analyze co-location patterns: where do entire-home listings cluster relative to income levels? Where do multi-listing hosts appear in relation to rent-burdened areas?

If we relied only on InsideAirbnb, Los Angeles would appear as a marketplace of listings. If we relied only on the ACS, it would appear as static demographic zones. By combining them, we analyze how a digital platform economy overlaps and interacts with long-term community structures — helping us answer: How does Airbnb spatially shape and commodify communities in Los Angeles, and for whom?
